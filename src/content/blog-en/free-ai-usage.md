AI tools are great. But subscriptions add up.

ChatGPT Plus: $20/month. Claude Pro: $20/month. Gemini Advanced: $20/month.

Before you know it, $60+ monthly for AI tools.

I didn't want to spend that much. Found ways to use AI effectively on budget. Sharing my experience.

## Free Tiers Are Actually Good

First realization: free tiers cover most needs.

**ChatGPT Free:**
- GPT-3.5 unlimited
- GPT-4 limited access
- Good enough for most tasks

**Claude Free:**
- Claude 3.5 Sonnet with daily limits
- Roughly 10-20 messages daily
- Sufficient for many users

**Gemini Free:**
- Gemini Pro access
- Web search integration
- Google Workspace integration

For casual use, free tiers work. I started with free everything.

## My Current Setup (Almost Free)

I now pay for one service: Claude Pro ($20/month).

Everything else: free tiers.

Here's how I make it work:

**Writing tasks: Claude Pro**

Long-form writing needs Claude. The quality difference matters for articles.

Claude's free tier limits would interrupt my workflow. So I pay.

**Quick questions: ChatGPT Free**

For simple questions, brainstorming, quick tasks, ChatGPT Free works fine.

GPT-3.5 is fast and capable. For many tasks, indistinguishable from GPT-4.

**Research: Perplexity Free**

Perplexity's free tier is generous. Real-time search with sources.

Better than ChatGPT for current information.

**Coding help: Claude or ChatGPT**

Both work for coding. I use Claude Pro since I have it.

If I didn't have Claude Pro, ChatGPT Free would cover basic coding help.

## Tactics to Save Money

**Use multiple free tiers:**

Don't stick to one tool. Use each for its strengths.

ChatGPT free for quick questions. Claude free for daily writing tasks. Perplexity for research.

Combined, they cover most needs.

**Know the limits:**

Claude free: ~10-20 messages/day
ChatGPT free: GPT-4 limited, GPT-3.5 unlimited
Gemini free: generous limits

Plan your usage. Save important tasks for the best model. Use others for simple tasks.

**API instead of subscription:**

If you're technical, consider API access.

Claude API: pay only for what you use. For light users, cheaper than subscription.

ChatGPT API: similar, pay per use.

If you use AI heavily, subscription is cheaper. If lightly, API might save money.

**Use open-source models:**

Llama, Mistral, others are free. Run them locally.

Requires:
- Technical knowledge
- Good hardware
- Setup time

But once running, unlimited free use.

I use Llama locally for private tasks. Claude for work tasks. No extra cost.

**Team/family sharing:**

Some services allow multiple users on one account.

ChatGPT Team, Claude Team. Split cost among users.

Check terms carefully. Some personal accounts don't allow sharing.

## When Free Isn't Enough

Free tiers have limits. When do you need paid?

**Heavy daily use:**

If you hit free tier limits daily, paid makes sense.

I was hitting Claude limits constantly. Paying saved frustration.

**Work-critical tasks:**

If AI is essential for work, reliability matters.

Free tiers can have outages, slow during peak times, or limit access.

Paid tiers have priority, more reliability.

**Need best models:**

Free tiers often use older models or limit access to best models.

If you need GPT-4 consistently or Claude 3.5 Opus, paid is necessary.

**API access:**

If you're building applications, API access is required.

Can't use free web interfaces programmatically.

## Free Alternatives Worth Knowing

**Chatbot Arena:**

Lmsys.org's Chatbot Arena lets you test models for free.

Various top models available. Not for daily use, but for occasional testing.

**Poe:**

Quora's Poe offers multiple models. Some free access to different AIs.

Limited but useful for trying different models.

**Hugging Face:**

Many open-source models available for free use. Quality varies.

Good for experimentation and specific tasks.

**Local models:**

Llama, Mistral, others run locally. Free unlimited use.

Requires hardware and setup. But truly free after that.

## Strategies for Different Use Cases

**Light user (few times weekly):**

Free tiers only. No need for paid.

ChatGPT Free covers most needs.

**Moderate user (daily):**

One paid subscription for primary need. Free tiers for rest.

My setup: Claude Pro paid, others free.

**Heavy user (all day, work):**

Multiple subscriptions might be worth it. Or API access.

Calculate: subscription vs API based on usage volume.

**Developer:**

API access makes sense. Pay for what you use.

Or local models for privacy and cost control.

## Signs You Should Pay

**You hit limits weekly:**

If you constantly hit free tier limits, your time is worth more than the subscription cost.

**Quality matters significantly:**

If GPT-4 vs GPT-3.5 affects your work output, the upgrade pays for itself.

**AI is essential for income:**

If AI helps you earn, subscription is a business expense.

One hour of frustration costs more than $20 subscription.

**You need privacy:**

Some free tiers may use conversations for training. Paid tiers often offer more privacy.

## Signs You Don't Need Paid

**You forget to use AI:**

If AI isn't part of your workflow, free tier is fine.

**Free tier meets needs:**

If you're not hitting limits, don't fix what isn't broken.

**Budget is tight:**

$20/month isn't trivial. If budget is tight, free tiers work.

**You're still exploring:**

If you haven't found your AI use case, don't commit. Test with free tiers first.

## My Advice

**Start free:**

Use free tiers first. Understand your actual needs.

I used only free tiers for 6 months. Then understood which service I used most.

**Pay for what you use most:**

My primary use is writing. Claude is best for writing. So I pay for Claude.

Your needs may differ. Pay for the tool you use most.

**Don't pay for everything:**

Subscription fatigue is real. $20 here, $20 there, suddenly $100/month for software.

Be selective. Pay for what provides value. Free for the rest.

**Reevaluate regularly:**

Every few months, check usage. Are you using the paid service? Could free work?

Services change. Needs change. Don't auto-renew without thought.

## The Bottom Line

AI tools are valuable. But subscriptions add up.

My approach: one paid service for core needs, free tiers for everything else.

$20/month covers 95% of my AI needs.

You don't need every AI subscription. You need the right subscription.

Start free. Understand your needs. Pay strategically.
