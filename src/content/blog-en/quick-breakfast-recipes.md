Morning rush. No time. Skip breakfast or grab something unhealthy.

I used to do this every day. Then wonder why I was hungry by 10am, or sluggish all morning.

These 5 breakfast recipes changed my mornings. All take 15 minutes or less. All are nutritious. All keep me full until lunch.

## 1. Avocado Toast with Egg (10 minutes)

My go-to breakfast. Protein, healthy fats, complex carbs. Keeps me full for hours.

**Ingredients:**
- 2 slices whole grain bread
- 1/2 avocado
- 2 eggs
- Salt, pepper
- Optional: red pepper flakes, everything bagel seasoning

**Instructions:**

1. Toast bread (2 minutes)
2. While toasting, mash avocado in a bowl with salt and pepper (2 minutes)
3. In a pan, fry eggs to your preference (4 minutes)
4. Spread avocado on toast, top with eggs (1 minute)
5. Add seasonings

Total time: 9 minutes

**Nutrition:**
- Protein: ~15g
- Healthy fats: ~20g
- Fiber: ~6g
- Calories: ~350

**Make it faster:**

Boil eggs at the start of the week. Use pre-boiled eggs, mash on avocado toast. Cuts time to 5 minutes.

**Variations:**

- Add sliced tomatoes
- Top with microgreens
- Use smoked salmon instead of egg
- Add crumbled feta cheese

## 2. Greek Yogurt Parfait (5 minutes)

No cooking required. Prep the night before for even faster mornings.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup Greek yogurt (plain)
- 1/2 cup berries (fresh or frozen)
- 2 tbsp granola or nuts
- 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup

**Instructions:**

1. In a jar or bowl, layer half the yogurt (1 minute)
2. Add half the berries (30 seconds)
3. Add half the granola (30 seconds)
4. Repeat layers
5. Drizzle with honey

Total time: 4 minutes

**Nutrition:**
- Protein: ~20g
- Fiber: ~5g
- Calories: ~300

**Make it faster:**

Assemble the night before. Keep granola separate and add in the morning to stay crunchy.

**Variations:**

- Use different fruits: banana, apple, mango
- Add chia seeds for extra fiber
- Use different nuts: almonds, walnuts, pecans
- Add cinnamon for flavor

## 3. Veggie Scramble (12 minutes)

Get vegetables in at breakfast. Protein-packed and filling.

**Ingredients:**
- 3 eggs
- 1 cup mixed vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms)
- 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
- Salt, pepper
- Optional: cheese, herbs

**Instructions:**

1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat (1 minute)
2. Add vegetables, sauté until soft (4 minutes)
3. While vegetables cook, beat eggs in a bowl (1 minute)
4. Pour eggs over vegetables (1 minute)
5. Scramble until cooked through (4 minutes)
6. Season to taste

Total time: 11 minutes

**Nutrition:**
- Protein: ~18g
- Fiber: ~3g
- Calories: ~280

**Make it faster:**

Pre-chop vegetables on Sunday. Store in containers. Morning prep becomes dump and cook.

**Variations:**

- Add diced tomatoes
- Use different vegetables based on season
- Top with hot sauce
- Add leftover cooked meat (chicken, turkey)

## 4. Overnight Oats (5 minutes prep night before)

The ultimate morning time-saver. Prep the night before, grab and go in the morning.

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup milk (or milk alternative)
- 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
- 1/2 cup fruit (banana, berries, apple)

**Instructions (Night Before):**

1. Mix oats, milk, yogurt, chia seeds, and sweetener in a jar (2 minutes)
2. Stir well (30 seconds)
3. Add fruit on top (1 minute)
4. Cover and refrigerate overnight

Morning: Grab and go. Total morning time: 0 minutes.

**Nutrition:**
- Protein: ~15g
- Fiber: ~8g
- Calories: ~350

**Variations:**

- Chocolate: add cocoa powder and top with banana
- Peanut butter banana: add peanut butter and banana
- Apple cinnamon: add diced apple and cinnamon
- Berry: mixed berries and vanilla extract

**Make 5 at once:**

Sunday night, prepare 5 jars for the week. They keep for 5 days in the fridge.

## 5. Breakfast Wrap (8 minutes)

Portable breakfast for eating on the go. Warm, filling, satisfying.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 large tortilla (whole wheat)
- 2 eggs (or 1/2 cup egg whites)
- 1/4 cup black beans
- 1/4 cup shredded cheese
- 2 tbsp salsa
- Optional: avocado, spinach

**Instructions:**

1. Scramble eggs in a pan (4 minutes)
2. Add beans to warm through (1 minute)
3. Lay tortilla flat, add eggs, beans, cheese, salsa (1 minute)
4. Fold into a wrap (1 minute)
5. Optional: heat in pan to melt cheese and crisp tortilla (1 minute)

Total time: 8 minutes

**Nutrition:**
- Protein: ~22g
- Fiber: ~6g
- Calories: ~380

**Make it faster:**

Scramble eggs at the start of the week. Store in fridge. Morning assembly takes 3 minutes.

**Variations:**

- Add cooked bacon or sausage
- Use different beans: pinto, kidney
- Add sautéed vegetables
- Use different cheese: cheddar, pepper jack

## Weekly Prep Strategy

The key to fast breakfasts is prep. Sunday prep = easy weekday mornings.

**What to prep Sunday:**

**Eggs:**
- Boil 6-8 eggs. Store in fridge for quick breakfasts or snacks.
- Scramble a batch of eggs. Store for breakfast wraps.

**Vegetables:**
- Chop onions, peppers, mushrooms, spinach. Store in containers.
- Use for scrambles, wraps, or omelets throughout the week.

**Overnight Oats:**
- Prepare 5 jars at once. Ready for each morning.

**Fruit:**
- Wash berries. Slice melon. Store for easy access.
- Freeze bananas for smoothies.

**Beans and Grains:**
- Cook a batch of beans. Use for wraps, bowls.
- Cook quinoa or rice for breakfast bowls.

**Time investment:** 1 hour on Sunday. Saves 10-15 minutes each weekday morning.

## What About Smoothies?

Smoothies are quick and healthy. But I didn't include them as a main recipe for a reason:

**Problems with smoothies:**

- Liquid calories don't satisfy as long as solid food
- Easy to overdo fruit (too much sugar)
- Not as filling as protein + fiber solid food
- Can spike blood sugar, leading to mid-morning crash

**If you love smoothies:**

Add protein powder, nut butter, or Greek yogurt for protein and satiety. Use more vegetables than fruit. Add fiber (chia seeds, flax seeds).

Smoothies can work, but I find solid breakfasts keep me fuller longer.

## Common Questions

**"I'm not hungry in the morning."**

Start small. A piece of fruit, a handful of nuts. Gradually your appetite will adjust.

Morning hunger is trainable. If you never eat breakfast, your body learns not to expect it. Start eating small amounts, hunger will develop.

**"I really have zero time."**

Overnight oats or pre-prepared yogurt parfaits require zero morning time. Grab from fridge, eat at your desk.

Prep on weekends, grab during week. No excuses.

**"These aren't enough calories."**

Adjust portions. Add more eggs, bigger portion of yogurt, extra toppings. These recipes are base templates.

**"I need something sweet."**

The overnight oats and yogurt parfait can satisfy sweet cravings. Add more fruit or a bit more honey/maple.

**"I get hungry by 10am."**

Add more protein and fiber. Eggs keep you fuller than cereal. Greek yogurt has more protein than regular yogurt. The combos in these recipes are designed for satiety.
