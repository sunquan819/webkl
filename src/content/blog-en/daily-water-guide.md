Used to think drinking water was simple. Thirsty, drink water. Done.

Later realized timing matters. Right times to drink water, body absorbs better, more benefits.

Did some research, adjusted habits. Now my water schedule works great. Sharing what I learned.

## Why Timing Matters

Body has natural rhythms. Metabolism, digestion, energy levels all change throughout day.

Drinking water at right times:
- Helps metabolism
- Improves digestion
- Maintains energy
- Supports detox

Random drinking still works, but strategic drinking works better.

## Morning: First Glass After Waking

After 7-8 hours sleep, body is dehydrated. Lost water through breathing and sweating overnight.

First thing after waking, drink a glass of water.

**Benefits:**
- Rehydrates body after sleep
- Wakes up digestive system
- Helps metabolism start
- Clears mind faster

**My routine:**
Before breakfast, one glass of warm water. Not cold, not hot. Warm water gentler on empty stomach.

Been doing this for a year. Morning energy noticeably better, digestion improved too.

**Amount:** About 250-300ml (one glass). Don't overdo it.

## Before Meals: 30 Minutes Prior

Drinking water before meals helps digestion and controls appetite.

**30 minutes before eating:**
One glass of water. This prepares stomach for food, helps digestive enzymes work better.

**Why 30 minutes before?**
If you drink right before eating, water dilutes stomach acid. Not good for digestion. 30 minutes gives water time to absorb, doesn't interfere with digestion.

**Appetite control:**
Sometimes thirst feels like hunger. Drinking water first, if hunger goes away, was actually thirst not hunger.

This helps avoid unnecessary snacking.

## During Meals: Small Sips Only

During meals, sip a little water. Not large amounts.

**Why small amounts:**
Large amounts of water during meals dilute stomach acid and digestive enzymes. Food doesn't digest as well.

**My approach:**
Eat mostly without water. If food is dry, small sips help. But no large glasses with meals.

This took adjustment. Used to drink a lot while eating. Now eat first, drink later. Digestion much better.

## After Meals: Wait One Hour

After eating, wait at least one hour before drinking lots of water.

**Why wait?**
Stomach needs time to digest. Water immediately after meals rushes through, taking food with it before proper digestion.

**What I do:**
Eat dinner at 7pm, drink water at 8pm or later. Gives stomach time to work.

Small sips okay, but avoid big glasses right after meals.

## Afternoon Energy Dip: Water Helps

Around 2-3pm, often feel tired. Used to grab coffee.

Now I drink water first.

**Why:**
Afternoon tiredness often from mild dehydration, not lack of caffeine.

Drinking water around 2pm:
- Rehydrates body
- Improves energy
- Helps focus

**My routine:**
2pm, one glass of water. If still tired, then consider coffee. Usually water is enough.

## Before Exercise: 30 Minutes Prior

Before exercise, drink water 30 minutes ahead.

**Why:**
Exercise causes sweating, losing water. Pre-hydrating ensures body has water reserves.

**Amount:**
300-500ml, 30 minutes before. Enough to hydrate, not so much you feel full.

**During exercise:**
Small sips every 15-20 minutes. Don't wait until thirsty.

## Evening: Reduce After 8pm

Evening water intake should decrease.

**Why:**
Drinking too much at night means bathroom trips, disrupted sleep.

**My rule:**
After 8pm, minimal water. If thirsty, small sips only.

**Exception:**
If exercise late, obviously need to rehydrate. Just not right before bed.

## Before Bed: Optional Small Amount

Some say drink before bed, others say don't.

**My take:**
If you wake up thirsty at night, drink a little before bed. If you don't, skip it.

Small amount, 100ml max. Not enough to cause bathroom trips.

I personally skip this. My body doesn't need it. Listen to your body.

## How Much Water Total?

General recommendation: 2-2.5 liters daily.

But this varies by:
- Body size
- Activity level
- Climate
- Diet

**My approach:**
Don't obsess over numbers. Check urine color:
- Clear or light yellow: hydrated enough
- Dark yellow: need more water
- Completely clear: drinking too much

Body signals are more accurate than counting glasses.

## What to Drink

Plain water best. Sometimes add lemon for taste.

**Avoid:**
- Sugary drinks (juice, soda)
- Too much coffee (dehydrates)
- Alcohol (dehydrates)

Herbal tea counts as water. Coffee partly counts but not fully.

## Temperature: Warm vs Cold

This is debated.

**My experience:**
Warm water feels better for digestion. Cold water shocks stomach.

Especially on empty stomach, warm water much gentler.

Room temperature works too. Avoid ice water.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Chugging large amounts**
Body can only absorb about 200ml every 15 minutes. Chugging a liter at once mostly passes through without absorption.

Better to sip slowly throughout day.

**Mistake 2: Only drinking when thirsty**
Thirst means you're already dehydrated. Drink regularly before feeling thirsty.

**Mistake 3: Ignoring water quality**
If tap water doesn't taste good, you won't want to drink it. Use filtered water if needed.

**Mistake 4: Drinking with meals**
As mentioned, large amounts with meals hurt digestion. Wait before and after.

## My Daily Water Schedule

Here's my routine:

- 7:00am: One glass warm water after waking
- 10:00am: One glass water
- 12:00pm: One glass water (30 min before lunch)
- 3:00pm: One glass water
- 6:00pm: One glass water (30 min before dinner)
- 8:00pm: One glass water (last big glass)

Total about 1.5-2 liters. Adjust based on activity and weather.

## Benefits I Noticed

After following this schedule for months:

- Morning energy improved
- Digestion better
- Afternoon tiredness reduced
- Skin looks healthier
- Fewer headaches

Water isn't magic. But proper hydration makes body work better.

## Start Simple

If you're not used to drinking water regularly, start simple:

1. One glass after waking (non-negotiable)
2. One glass before each meal
3. One glass afternoon

These three habits already cover most needs. Once comfortable, add more timing.

Don't overcomplicate. Basic timing, consistent habit, results come.
