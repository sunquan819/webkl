Weight loss has been something I struggled with for years.

I tried starvation diets, diet pills, trendy weight loss methods. Results? Either lost weight and regained it, or nothing happened at all.

Last year I decided to lose weight seriously. Three months later, I lost 20 lbs. A year has passed, and the weight stayed off. No rebound.

Here's how I did it. Not magic methods, just simple changes that actually work.

## Why My Previous Attempts Failed

Let me share my failed attempts first, so you can avoid the same mistakes.

**Starvation Diet:**

At first I tried starvation. Only two meals a day, each meal tiny portions.

Initially I did lose weight - 5 lbs in two weeks. Then it rebounded, faster than I lost it.

The worse part was how terrible I felt. No energy, no focus, work productivity dropped. When I returned to normal eating, weight came back plus 3 extra lbs.

Later I learned: starvation puts your body into "famine mode," lowering metabolism. When you resume eating, your body stores energy aggressively. That's the rapid rebound.

**Diet Pills:**

Then I tried diet pills. Ads promised "lose weight while sleeping."

Two months later, I lost 8 lbs. But side effects were terrible: insomnia, heart racing, dry mouth. After stopping, weight rebounded, and my health felt worse.

Most diet pills suppress appetite or boost metabolism. Both harm your body. I never touch diet pills anymore.

**Trendy Methods:**

Meal replacements, enzymes, detox programs - I tried them all.

Effect? Some had short-term results, but nothing lasting. These methods are either expensive, inconvenient, or harmful.

After all this trial and error, I concluded: weight loss can't rely on "quick fixes." It needs lifestyle changes.

## How I Actually Lost the Weight

This time, I changed my approach: no quick results, focus on sustainability.

**Step 1: Change Food Composition**

I didn't starve. Three meals a day, normal eating. But changed what I ate:

- Breakfast: eggs + whole wheat bread + milk. Previously fried dough + soy milk. Now high protein, low GI combo.
- Lunch: Normal meal but half the rice, double vegetables, meat stays same.
- Dinner: Mostly vegetables, some protein, minimal carbs.

Key is protein at every meal. Protein increases satiety, reduces snack cravings.

Before I always wanted afternoon snacks. Now with enough protein at breakfast and lunch, I rarely get hungry in the afternoon.

**Step 2: Cut Snacks and Drinks**

This was hardest, but most effective.

Before I had milk tea or snacks every afternoon. One milk tea = 300+ calories, one afternoon snack set = 500+ calories. These "small things" add up to hundreds of calories daily.

After cutting snacks and drinks, I reduced 500+ calories daily. Monthly reduction: 15,000+ calories, roughly 4 lbs lost.

Method is simple: don't buy snacks. No snacks at home or office. When hungry, drink water or eat fruit.

Milk tea was hardest. I used to have one daily. Then weekly, then monthly, then stopped completely.

Now occasionally I try one, it tastes too sweet, don't really want it anymore.

**Step 3: Walk One Hour Daily**

No gym, no high intensity exercise. Just walking one hour daily.

Walking is simple. No equipment needed, no cost, can do anywhere anytime.

My routine: walk after dinner, about 60 minutes, moderate pace. Better than sitting.

Walking one hour burns roughly 200-300 calories. Plus the 500 calories saved from snacks, daily deficit of 700+ calories.

Three months later, plus diet changes, I lost 20 lbs.

**Step 4: Early Sleep, Early Wake**

Weight loss isn't just eating and moving. Sleep matters too.

I used to stay up late, sleeping after midnight. Sleep deprivation increases appetite and lowers metabolism.

Changed to sleep before 11pm, wake at 7am. 7-8 hours sleep daily.

With enough sleep, daytime energy is good, less desire to eat, metabolism more normal.

## Important Details

**Don't weigh yourself weekly:**

Initially I weighed weekly. Sometimes no change for a week, very anxious.

Changed to monthly weighing. Better mindset, not affected by short-term fluctuations.

Weight loss is long-term process. Weekly fluctuations don't matter. Focus on long-term trend.

**What to do at plateau:**

During three months, there was a phase with no weight change for two weeks.

Anxious at the time, but knew this was normal. Body adjusting. Patience and persistence.

Two weeks later, weight started dropping again. So don't give up at plateau.

**Don't completely cut carbs:**

Some people completely cut carbs, only protein and vegetables.

I tried this. Faster results, but felt exhausted, brain not working, mood terrible.

Changed to half carbs, not zero. Body state good, weight loss sustainable.

## Current Status

Three months weight loss, 20 lbs down. One year passed, weight stable, no rebound.

Because I didn't starve, didn't use extreme methods, returning to normal life kept weight stable.

Now occasionally eat snacks, occasionally have milk tea, but amounts controlled. Most time maintain the weight loss eating habits.

The hardest part isn't methods, it's persistence. I persisted three months. Sometimes wanted to give up, but remembering previous failures kept me going.

## Suggestions for Beginners

1. Don't pursue speed, pursue sustainability. 2 lbs per week beats 2 lbs per day.

2. Don't starve. Change food composition. Eat everything but control amounts.

3. Cut snacks and drinks. Most obvious effect, easiest to do.

4. Move daily. No high intensity needed, walking works.

5. Sleep enough. Sleep affects appetite and metabolism, don't stay up late.

6. Don't be anxious. Weight fluctuations are normal, long-term persistence matters.
