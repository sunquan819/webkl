ETFs make investing simple. One fund, instant diversification.

Here are 10 US ETFs I'm watching in 2026. Not recommendations, just what I have my eye on.

## 1. VOO - Vanguard S&P 500 ETF

The classic. Tracks the S&P 500, 500 largest US companies.

Expense ratio: 0.03%. Extremely cheap.

Why I like it: Simple exposure to US large caps. Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia - all included. My core holding.

If you only buy one ETF, this or similar S&P 500 fund is solid.

## 2. QQQ - Invesco Nasdaq 100 ETF

Tech-heavy. Top holdings: Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, Tesla.

Expense ratio: 0.20%.

Why I'm watching: Tech still drives innovation. AI, cloud, semiconductors - Nasdaq companies lead.

But tech can be volatile. QQQ drops more in downturns, rises more in upturns.

## 3. VTI - Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF

Broader than S&P 500. Includes small and mid-cap stocks too.

Expense ratio: 0.03%.

Why I like it: Total US market exposure. About 4000 stocks. More diversification than S&P 500.

If you want one fund for complete US stock exposure, VTI covers it.

## 4. SCHD - Schwab US Dividend Equity ETF

Focus on dividend-paying stocks. Good for income.

Expense ratio: 0.06%.

Why I'm watching: Dividend stocks tend to be more stable. SCHD holds established companies that pay dividends regularly.

Yield around 3-4%. If market drops, you still get dividends.

## 5. IWM - iShares Russell 2000 ETF

Small cap stocks. Russell 2000 index.

Expense ratio: 0.19%.

Why I'm watching: Small caps have underperformed recently. But historically, small caps outperform long-term.

If economy recovers strongly, small caps might lead. Riskier, but potential for higher returns.

## 6. VIG - Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF

Companies with history of increasing dividends.

Expense ratio: 0.06%.

Why I like it: These are quality companies. Raising dividends consistently requires strong business.

Lower yield than SCHD, but dividend growth is attractive.

## 7. XLE - Energy Select Sector SPDR Fund

Energy stocks: oil, gas, etc.

Expense ratio: 0.10%.

Why I'm watching: Energy sector has been volatile. If oil prices rise, XLE benefits.

Sector bets are risky. But XLE provides exposure if you want energy plays.

## 8. GLD - SPDR Gold Shares

Gold ETF. Tracks gold price.

Expense ratio: 0.40%.

Why I'm watching: Gold as hedge against uncertainty. When stocks drop, gold sometimes rises.

Not for growth, but for diversification. Small allocation can reduce portfolio risk.

## 9. BND - Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF

Bond ETF. US bonds.

Expense ratio: 0.03%.

Why I'm watching: Bonds provide stability. When stocks crash, bonds often hold value or rise.

Yield around 4-5% currently. Income plus lower volatility.

## 10. VXUS - Vanguard Total International Stock ETF

Non-US stocks. Europe, Asia, emerging markets.

Expense ratio: 0.07%.

Why I'm watching: US stocks aren't everything. International diversification matters.

Non-US stocks have underperformed US recently. But mean reversion could happen.

## How I Think About These ETFs

These 10 aren't recommendations. They're starting points for research.

My approach:

**Core Holdings:**

VOO or VTI for US stocks. BND for bonds. VXUS for international.

This trio gives broad exposure. Simple, effective.

**Satellite Positions:**

QQQ for tech exposure. SCHD for dividends. IWM for small caps.

Smaller allocations, more targeted.

**Occasional Plays:**

XLE for energy bets. GLD for hedging.

Only when I have a specific thesis. Not permanent holdings.

## What to Consider in 2026

Interest rates, inflation, economic growth - all affect ETF performance.

**Interest Rates:**

Higher rates hurt bonds, potentially slow stocks. Lower rates boost both.

Watch Fed policy.

**Inflation:**

High inflation hurts stocks and bonds. But commodities and real assets might benefit.

**Economic Growth:**

Strong growth helps stocks, especially small caps. Recession hurts stocks.

Nobody knows what 2026 brings. That's why diversification matters.

## Fees Matter

Compare expense ratios. VOO at 0.03% costs $3 annually per $10,000 invested.

Some funds charge 0.5% or more. That's $50 annually per $10,000.

Over decades, fee differences compound. Choose low-cost funds when possible.

## Final Thoughts

ETFs make investing accessible. Low fees, instant diversification, simple to understand.

The 10 ETFs above cover most needs. Core index funds, sector plays, bonds, gold, international.

Don't overthink it. Pick a few core ETFs, stay diversified, invest regularly.

Time in the market beats timing the market. Start early, stay consistent.
