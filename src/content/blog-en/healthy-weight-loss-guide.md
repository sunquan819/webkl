I tried starving myself to lose weight. It worked for a week, then I binged and regained everything plus more.

Learned the hard way: sustainable weight loss isn't about suffering.

## Why Starvation Diets Fail

Your body is smart. When you dramatically cut calories, it adapts.

Metabolism slows down. You burn fewer calories. Your body thinks there's a famine, conserves energy.

Then when you eat normally again, your body stores extra fat, preparing for the next "famine."

Result: you end up heavier than before.

Starvation diets create a cycle: restrict, binge, restrict, binge. Each cycle, weight goes up.

I did this for years. Never got anywhere.

## A Better Approach

The key is creating a moderate calorie deficit. Not huge, just enough.

Your body doesn't panic. Metabolism stays normal. Weight comes off slowly but sustainably.

**Calculate Your Needs:**

First, estimate how many calories you burn daily.

Simple formula: body weight in pounds × 12-14 for sedentary people, × 15-17 for active.

If you weigh 180 pounds and are mostly sedentary: 180 × 13 = 2340 calories daily maintenance.

**Create a Moderate Deficit:**

Subtract 500 calories for weight loss. 2340 - 500 = 1840 calories.

This creates about 1 pound of weight loss per week. Slow but sustainable.

Don't go below 1200 calories for women, 1500 for men. Too low creates problems.

**Track for a While:**

Track what you eat for a few weeks. Use an app. It's eye-opening.

I thought I was eating 1800 calories. Actually eating 2500. Tracking revealed the truth.

After a few weeks, you'll learn to estimate portions. Then you can stop tracking.

## What to Eat

Calories matter most. But food quality affects hunger, energy, and health.

**Prioritize Protein:**

Protein is most satiating. High protein diets help you feel full with fewer calories.

Aim for 0.8-1 gram protein per pound of body weight. 180 pound person = 144-180 grams protein.

Good sources: chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, lean beef, tofu, legumes.

**Eat Vegetables:**

Vegetables are low calorie, high volume. You can eat a lot and stay in your calorie limit.

Half your plate should be vegetables. They fill you up.

**Don't Fear Carbs:**

Carbs aren't evil. They're energy.

Just choose better sources: whole grains, fruits, starchy vegetables. Limit refined carbs like white bread, sugar, pastries.

Carbs around workouts help performance. I eat most of my carbs before and after exercise.

**Healthy Fats:**

Fats are calorie-dense but important for health. Include moderate amounts.

Avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish. Good for you, just watch portions.

A handful of nuts is fine. The whole bag is not.

## What I Actually Eat

Here's a typical day for me. Around 1800-2000 calories.

**Breakfast:**

Two eggs scrambled with vegetables, slice of whole wheat toast, piece of fruit.

About 350 calories. Keeps me full until lunch.

**Lunch:**

Large salad with grilled chicken, vegetables, olive oil dressing. Maybe some quinoa.

About 500 calories. Big volume, satisfying.

**Snack:**

Greek yogurt with berries. Sometimes a handful of almonds.

About 200 calories.

**Dinner:**

Protein (chicken, fish, or beef), large portion of vegetables, moderate portion of carbs.

About 600 calories.

**Evening:**

Sometimes a small treat. Dark chocolate, fruit, occasional cookie.

About 150 calories for treats.

Total: around 1800 calories. Never feel deprived.

## Movement Matters

Diet drives weight loss. Exercise drives health and helps maintain weight.

You can't out-exercise a bad diet. But exercise supports weight loss.

**Walk More:**

Walking is underrated. 8000-10000 steps daily makes a difference.

I walk 30 minutes after lunch and dinner. 60 minutes total. Burns 200-300 calories, aids digestion.

**Strength Training:**

Build muscle, burn more calories at rest. Plus, you look better.

Three times per week, 45 minutes. Not complicated. Basic exercises: squats, deadlifts, presses, rows.

Muscle tissue burns calories. More muscle = higher metabolism.

**Cardio:**

I do moderate cardio 2-3 times per week. Jogging, biking, swimming.

30-45 minutes. Not intense, just consistent movement.

## Habits That Helped

Beyond diet and exercise, certain habits made weight loss easier.

**Meal Prep:**

I cook in batches. Sunday, I prepare lunch for the week.

No decisions during the week. Just grab and eat. No temptation to order takeout.

**Sleep:**

Poor sleep increases hunger and cravings. When I sleep 7-8 hours, weight loss is easier.

I used to stay up late. Now I prioritize sleep.

**Drink Water:**

Often thirst masquerades as hunger. I drink water first when I feel hungry.

If still hungry 20 minutes later, then I eat. Often I was just thirsty.

**Don't Keep Junk at Home:**

If junk food isn't in my house, I can't eat it. Simple.

I buy healthy foods. If I want a treat, I have to go get it. That extra effort usually stops me.

**Allow Flexibility:**

I eat well 80% of the time. 20% of the time, I enjoy myself.

One bad meal doesn't ruin progress. One bad week doesn't either.

Perfection isn't required. Consistency over time matters.

## What About Cheat Days?

I don't do cheat days. They trigger bingeing.

Instead, I allow small treats daily. A square of chocolate, a cookie, a small portion of ice cream.

Total restriction leads to rebellion. Moderation is sustainable.

## Common Mistakes

**Drinking Calories:**

Sodas, juices, fancy coffee drinks. Easy to consume 500+ calories daily without realizing.

I switched to water, black coffee, tea. Cut hundreds of calories effortlessly.

**Eating Too Little:**

Some people cut too aggressively. 1000 calories, 800 calories.

Unsustainable. Leads to bingeing, muscle loss, metabolic damage.

Moderate deficit. Be patient.

**Giving Up After One Bad Day:**

One bad day. Maybe 3000 calories. People think "I ruined it" and quit.

One day means nothing. Weight loss is about months and years, not days.

If you have a bad day, just return to normal eating next day. No drama.

## My Results

Started at 185 pounds. Goal: 160 pounds.

Took 5 months. Lost about 1 pound per week.

Never felt starved. Never felt deprived. Ate foods I enjoyed.

Weight has stayed off for over a year now. Because I didn't do a crash diet, I built sustainable habits.

## Final Thoughts

Healthy weight loss isn't complicated. It's just not easy.

Create a moderate calorie deficit. Eat mostly whole foods. Move your body. Be patient.

The hard part is consistency. Not for a week, not for a month, but for as long as it takes.

You don't need to suffer. You don't need to starve. You need sustainable habits you can maintain forever.
