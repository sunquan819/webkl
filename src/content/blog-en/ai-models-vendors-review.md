Used AI tools for over a year now. Tried all major vendors: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and others.

Each has strengths and weaknesses. Sharing my real experience with each.

## OpenAI (ChatGPT)

OpenAI is the one I use most. Started with ChatGPT when it launched.

**Strengths:**

Fast. Responses come quickly. When you need quick answers, this matters.

Versatile. ChatGPT handles many tasks well: writing, coding, research, brainstorming. Not the best at everything, but good at everything.

Creative. Brainstorming sessions with ChatGPT often surprise me. Ideas are interesting and varied.

Wide knowledge base. General questions, ChatGPT usually has an answer.

**Weaknesses:**

Gets messy with long content. Writing long articles, it loses track. Paragraphs disconnected, logic drifts.

Sometimes lazy. Ask for a list, gives minimal items. Ask for detail, gives surface-level responses.

Context limit. Long conversations, it forgets earlier parts.

**Best for:**
- Quick questions
- Brainstorming
- Short writing
- Coding help
- Research starting points

**Pricing:**
Free tier available. Plus $20/month for GPT-4 access.

## Anthropic (Claude)

Claude is my go-to for serious writing and document work.

**Strengths:**

Writing quality. Long articles, Claude maintains coherence. Stays on topic, logical structure.

Context window. 200K tokens is massive. Upload entire documents, papers, books. Claude processes them fully.

Thoughtful responses. Takes time to think. Answers feel considered, not rushed.

Nuanced understanding. Complex topics, Claude handles subtlety well.

**Weaknesses:**

Slower. Thoughtfulness comes at cost of speed. Not painfully slow, but noticeably slower than ChatGPT.

Creative ideas more conservative. Brainstorming, Claude's suggestions tend toward safe, conventional.

Free tier limits. Limited daily messages on free tier. Heavy users need paid plan.

**Best for:**
- Long-form writing
- Document analysis
- Research papers
- Summarization
- Complex reasoning

**Pricing:**
Free tier with limits. Pro $20/month.

## Google (Gemini)

Google's Gemini. Had high hopes given Google's resources.

**Strengths:**

Google integration. If you use Google Workspace, Gemini connects with Docs, Sheets, Gmail.

Real-time information. Gemini can search the web for current info. ChatGPT's knowledge has cutoff.

Multimodal. Images, code, text all in one.

**Weaknesses:**

Inconsistent quality. Sometimes great responses, sometimes strange outputs. Less predictable than ChatGPT or Claude.

Overly cautious. Refuses certain questions that other AIs answer fine.

Hallucination issues. In my experience, more confident wrong answers than competitors.

**Best for:**
- Google Workspace users
- Current events questions
- Multimodal tasks
- Integration with Google products

**Pricing:**
Free tier available. Advanced $20/month.

## Perplexity

Perplexity is different. It's AI-powered search.

**Strengths:**

Real-time web search. Actually searches the web and cites sources. This is huge.

Citations. Every claim has a source link. Verify easily.

Up-to-date. Not limited by training data. Current information.

**Weaknesses:**

Not for creative work. It's search, not a creative assistant.

Limited reasoning. Complex analysis not its strength.

**Best for:**
- Research with sources
- Current information
- Fact-checking
- Quick lookups

**Pricing:**
Free tier available. Pro $20/month.

## Meta (Llama)

Meta's open-source models. Used them through various platforms.

**Strengths:**

Open source. Free to use. Can run locally if you have hardware.

Good quality for open source. Not as good as GPT-4 or Claude, but surprisingly capable.

Fast. Lightweight models respond quickly.

**Weaknesses:**

Quality gap. Not quite at the level of top commercial models.

Setup complexity. Running locally requires technical knowledge.

**Best for:**
- Developers wanting local AI
- Privacy-sensitive applications
- Cost-conscious users
- Custom implementations

**Pricing:**
Free. Open source.

## Mistral

European AI company. Smaller but impressive models.

**Strengths:**

Efficient. Good performance with smaller model size.

Multilingual. Strong European language support.

Open weights. Many models available to run locally.

**Weaknesses:**

Less known. Fewer integrations and tools compared to big players.

**Best for:**
- European users
- Local deployment
- Efficiency-focused applications

## My Current Stack

Different tasks, different tools:

| Task | Tool | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Quick questions | ChatGPT | Fast, versatile |
| Long writing | Claude | Coherent, thoughtful |
| Document analysis | Claude | Large context window |
| Research with sources | Perplexity | Citations, real-time |
| Current events | Gemini or Perplexity | Web access |
| Coding help | ChatGPT or Claude | Both work well |
| Local/privacy | Llama | Open source |

## What I Learned

**No single AI does everything best.**

I used to look for "the best AI." Doesn't exist. Each excels at different things.

**Paid subscriptions are worth it if you use AI daily.**

Free tiers are fine for occasional use. Daily use, paid tiers remove limits and improve quality.

**Always verify important information.**

All AIs hallucinate. They sound confident even when wrong. For important facts, verify with other sources.

**Prompt quality matters.**

Same AI, different prompts, vastly different outputs. Learning to prompt well is a skill.

## Which to Choose?

**For most people:**
Start with ChatGPT free tier. Covers most needs.

**For writers:**
Claude for long-form work. ChatGPT for short pieces.

**For researchers:**
Perplexity for sourced research. Claude for paper analysis.

**For developers:**
ChatGPT or Claude for coding help. Llama for local deployment.

**For Google Workspace users:**
Gemini integrates naturally.

## Future Outlook

AI space moves fast. Today's leader may be tomorrow's follower.

What matters:
- Quality of outputs
- Ease of use
- Specific features you need
- Price

Brand loyalty makes no sense here. Use what works, switch when something better comes along.
