For years, I couldn't sleep. Mind racing, heart pounding, staring at the ceiling for hours.

Anxiety and insomnia fed each other. Couldn't sleep because anxious. Anxious because couldn't sleep.

Here's what I tried, and what actually worked.

## What Didn't Work

Let me start with failures. Maybe you've tried these too.

**Sleeping Pills:**

Doctor prescribed sleeping pills. They worked - knocked me out cold.

Problem: I built tolerance quickly. Needed higher doses. Then couldn't sleep without them.

Worst: the "hangover" effect. Groggy all next day. Brain fog. Not rested.

Coming off sleeping pills was awful. Rebound insomnia worse than before.

Verdict: Short-term emergency use only. Not a solution.

**Alcohol:**

A drink helped me fall asleep. Seemed to work.

But alcohol disrupts sleep quality. I'd wake at 3am, unable to fall back asleep.

Plus, dependency risk. Drinking every night isn't healthy.

Verdict: Made insomnia worse long-term.

**Staying in Bed:**

Advice says "stay in bed, rest even if you can't sleep."

For me, this made anxiety worse. Lying awake, mind racing, frustrated.

After an hour of not sleeping, I'd be angry. Then sleep was impossible.

Verdict: Counterproductive for me.

**Counting Sheep:**

Does this work for anyone? I tried. Gave up after a few sheep.

My mind would wander back to anxious thoughts immediately.

Verdict: Useless.

**Late Night Screen Time:**

Scrolling my phone in bed. Blue light, stimulating content.

Worst thing for sleep. But I did it anyway, night after night.

Verdict: Absolutely makes insomnia worse.

## What Somewhat Worked

These helped a little, but not completely.

**Melatonin:**

Melatonin helped me fall asleep faster. Non-addictive, no hangover.

But didn't help me stay asleep. I'd fall asleep, wake at 2am, still couldn't fall back asleep.

Verdict: Good for falling asleep, not for staying asleep.

**Exercise:**

Exercise definitely improved my sleep. Physically tired body sleeps better.

But exercising too late made sleep worse. Needed to exercise before 6pm.

Verdict: Helps, but timing matters.

**Avoiding Caffeine:**

No caffeine after noon. This helped somewhat.

I'm sensitive to caffeine. Even afternoon coffee affected sleep.

Verdict: Helpful but not sufficient.

**Reading Before Bed:**

Reading a book (paper, not screen) helped calm my mind.

But sometimes I'd get absorbed and read for hours. Then too alert to sleep.

Verdict: Good if time-limited.

## What Actually Worked

These are the things that made a real difference.

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I):**

This was the game-changer.

CBT-I is a structured program that addresses thoughts and behaviors affecting sleep.

Key components that helped me:

Sleep restriction: Only spending 6 hours in bed initially. Builds sleep drive. When you're finally allowed in bed, you sleep.

Stimulus control: Bed is only for sleep. No reading, no phone, no thinking. If awake 20 minutes, get up. Bed becomes associated with sleep.

Cognitive restructuring: Addressing anxious thoughts about sleep. "If I don't sleep tonight, I'll be a mess tomorrow" -> "I've survived bad nights before. I'll manage."

Took 6-8 weeks to really work. But effects lasted. Sleep improved dramatically.

I used an app (CBT-I Coach, free) and a workbook. Therapy with a professional would be even better.

**Consistent Sleep Schedule:**

Same wake time every day. Weekends included.

This was hard. But it reset my body clock.

Within 2 weeks of consistent wake times, I started getting sleepy at the same time each night.

Now I wake naturally around 7am, even without alarm.

**Morning Sunlight:**

Getting sunlight within an hour of waking helped immensely.

I take a 15-minute walk every morning. Sunlight (even on cloudy days) helps regulate circadian rhythm.

Sounds simple. But it mattered.

**Anxiety Treatment:**

The insomnia was partly a symptom of anxiety. Treating anxiety helped sleep.

I started therapy. Learned to manage anxious thoughts. Practiced mindfulness.

Medication (SSRIs) also helped. Not sleeping pills - antidepressants that treat anxiety.

With anxiety better managed, sleep improved naturally.

**Journaling Before Bed:**

Anxious thoughts kept me awake. Worrying about tomorrow, replaying today.

I started writing everything down before bed. Tomorrow's worries, today's problems, random thoughts.

Once written, my mind could let go. "It's on paper, I don't need to think about it."

Still do this. Takes 10 minutes, helps enormously.

**Body Scan Meditation:**

Lying in bed, I focus on each body part from toes to head. Relaxing each area.

If my mind wanders, I gently bring it back.

This occupies my mind just enough to prevent anxious thoughts, but not enough to stay awake.

Free guided body scans on YouTube. I still use them sometimes.

**Cool, Dark Room:**

Made my bedroom sleep-friendly.

Blackout curtains. Temperature around 65°F. Quiet or white noise.

Removed everything stimulating from bedroom. No TV, no work desk. Just bed and nightstand.

Environment matters more than I realized.

**Magnesium Glycinate:**

Tried various supplements. Magnesium glycinate was most helpful.

Takes the edge off anxiety, promotes relaxation.

Not a miracle, but part of the puzzle.

## My Current Routine

This is what I do now. Most nights, I sleep well.

**Morning:**
Wake at 7am, same time daily
15-minute walk outside
Healthy breakfast

**Day:**
Exercise before 6pm
No caffeine after noon
Manage stress, take breaks

**Evening:**
Light dinner by 7pm
No screens after 9pm
Journaling at 9:30pm
Body scan meditation in bed
Asleep by 10:30pm

**If I Can't Sleep:**
After 20 minutes, get up
Go to another room
Read or do something boring
Return to bed when sleepy
Repeat if needed

Most nights now, I fall asleep within 20 minutes. Wake up rested.

## What I Wish I Knew Earlier

Insomnia feeds on itself. The more you worry about sleep, the worse it gets.

What I know now:

Sleep will happen eventually. Your body needs sleep. It's inevitable.

One bad night isn't a disaster. You'll function.

Fixing sleep takes time. Weeks to months. Be patient.

Address the underlying anxiety. Sleep problems often have root causes.

CBT-I is evidence-based. It works. Why did I wait so long?

## Getting Help

If anxiety and insomnia are affecting your life, get help.

Therapy works. CBT-I is specifically designed for insomnia. Talk to your doctor.

I suffered for years before getting help. Wish I'd done it sooner.

There's no shame in seeking treatment. It's not weakness. It's health management.

---

Anxiety and insomnia are exhausting. I know.

But they're treatable. Not with quick fixes, but with sustained effort and the right approach.

What worked for me might not work exactly the same for you. But the principles - addressing anxiety, sleep hygiene, CBT techniques - have good evidence.

Don't give up. Sleep is possible.
